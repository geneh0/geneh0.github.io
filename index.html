Intro

Welcome! On a personal level, I'm a first generation Asian American, with roots in Laos and Vietnam. While my father and his family fled China and Vietnam during the Viet Nam War to escape the Communist regime, My maternal my maternal family still lives on opposite sides of the Mekong River separating Thailand and Laos. The lives of these family members are juxtaposed due to the regimes they live under, influencing their social determinants of health. My family has remained a large motivation to pursue work in health, hence my focus on global health.

I'm a graduate student in Public Health, concentrating in Epidemiology and Biostatistics. I somewhat recently discovered a passion for data science and am pursuing a certificate in Applied Data Science.

I'm a two-time Berkeley Bear, having graduated in 2019 and 2020, both with degrees from the School of Public Health. My graduate degree is from the 4+1 MPH program, where I completed my master's degree in the year after getting my B.A. Throughout my time at Berkeley, I worked as an instructional designer, creating courses for the Online MPH program.

I've previously worked in several private and public health organizations. Most recently, I worked as a consulting intern at Ramboll Environ, working with air pollutant modeling data in SQL and R. Before that, I interned with the Division of Communicable Disease Control and Prevention at the Alameda County Public Health Department (ACPHD), assisting with Hepatitis A vaccination campaigns among the house-less, neonatal Hepatitis B case investigation, and Shoo the Flu. I later moved on to serve as a consultant for the Alameda County Public Health Commission, leveraging my previous job to liaise with ACPHD.

Projects

Masters Thesis

The Effects of Acupuncture on Breast Cancer Patient Outcomes: A Systematic Review

I wrote this systematic review as part of my Epidemiology/Biostatistics capstone. It included both observational and randomized controlled trials to assess the impacts of acupuncture on breast cancer patient/survivor fatigue and quality of life measures. Ultimately, the results were inconclusive due to a small body of literature.

Predicting US County Political Preferences with Social Distancing Implementation during COVID-19

A final report for Principles and Techniques of Data Science that utilized scikit-learn to develop log-linear and logistic models to use the lag/lead time from a county's first COVID-19 case to the implementation of various social distancing measures. While our models had reasonable errors/accuracy, the coefficients themselves were quite small. Even though we didn't conduct any statistical significance testing, as it was outside the scope of the class, the small beta coefficients hint that they might not be significant ("clinically" or statistically). Furthermore, statistical interaction and confounding were also outside the scope of this project. We hope to be able to refine our model in future iterations of this project.

Is Maternal Age Associated with Preterm Birth?

A final project poster for Statistical Analysis of Categorical Data that conducted a logistic regression to assess the association between maternal age and preterm birth, controlling for confounders and interaction.
